Correlations Discovered between Health Risks, Age, and Income

There is positive correlation between the percentage of healthcare lacking and the percentage of people in poverty. Likewise, the percentages of obesity and smoke both have positive relationship with the percentage of people in poverty. This suggests that the percentage of poverty correlates positively with smoking, obesity, and healthcare lackage.

Household income has a negative relationship with smoking, obesity, and healthcare lackage. This makes sense, because poverty and household income are directly related to each other.

Further, age has a clear positive relationship with smoking, although its relationship with obesity is less clear. Age has a negative relationship with healcare lackage.
